Did you know, that most studies concluded that plastic bags are superior to paper bags in terms of immediate carbon emissions? Some studies have concluded that bioplastics have the potential for 
major greenhouse gas reductions, while others have found the opposite. The main difference between studies with opposite results for bioplastics has been the selection of waste treatment scenarios. 

Mattila, Tuomas, et al. “Uncertainty and Sensitivity in the Carbon Footprint of Shopping Bags.” 
Journal of Industrial Ecology, vol. 15, no. 2, 2011, pp. 217–27, https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1530-9290.2010.00326.x.
